# Casa Atlas — Pitch Index

## Reviewer Classification

### Category A1 — Architectural, Critical (super serious)
*These publications have editorial boards, critical voices, and institutional weight. They select for ideas, not images. A project submitted here must be able to sustain intellectual scrutiny — the concept must be arguable, the photography must be publication-grade, and ideally a written statement from Marcela accompanies it. Tone: rigorous, essay-level, no styling language.*

| Outlet | What they need | Status |
|---|---|---|
| `thearchitecturalreview` | A critical argument about what the project does architecturally. Ideally a short essay by Marcela. The project must stand on its own as a position, not just a beautiful house. | [ ] Draft |
| `_arquine` | Conceptual clarity + Marcela's voice on methodology. Less about the project, more about the practice's relationship to territory and listening. Essay format preferred. | [ ] Draft |

### Category A2 — Architectural, Accessible
*Still architecture-focused and disciplined, but image-led and more open to submissions. They value a clear design argument but don't require academic rigor. Good photography + a one-paragraph thesis is enough to get consideration. Tone: confident, precise, but not intimidating.*

| Outlet | What they need | Status |
|---|---|---|
| `the_best_new_architects` | One clear design idea stated plainly. Open submissions. Carlos Castillo-Cortes reads them personally. | [x] Draft |
| `worldarchitecturedesign` | Strong exterior/site images. Submit via email. No essay needed — the photographs carry it. | [x] Draft |
| `archpaper` | A news angle or practice hook. Journalistic tone. The Marcela-as-founder story or the Homística collaboration works as a hook. | [ ] Draft |
