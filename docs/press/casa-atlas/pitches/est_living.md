## Editorial Pitch Letter

Subject: Submission — Casa Atlas, Cerritos BCS — Architecture + Interiors by Oficio Taller + Estudio Homística

Dear Est Living editorial team,

We would like to submit Casa Atlas for feature consideration — a completed private residence in Cerritos, Baja California Sur, designed by Oficio Taller with interiors by Estudio Homística.

Atlas was designed to hold what its inhabitants bring to it: the salt, the fire, the bread.

The house sits behind a coastal dune in Cerritos, Baja California Sur — earth-toned concrete volumes that emerge from the ground and open, gradually, toward the sea. Walls the color of sand and mountain contain what is intimate and release it toward the horizon. The dune is shelter and threshold: between architecture and landscape, between inside and outside, between the private and the shared.

The house was designed for a chef and his family. The kitchen — there are two — is where it begins and ends. The upper kitchen opens entirely to the terrace, the pool, and the Pacific: you cook facing the ocean. The lower kitchen connects to an edible garden and an outdoor dining space oriented toward the dune. The rituals of cultivating, harvesting, cooking, serving, and sharing together are the architecture's organizing logic.

Interiors were developed in collaboration with Estudio Homística — a layering of Mexican craft and quiet restraint. Hand-thrown clay vessels, local wood furniture whose irregular grain celebrates the beauty of imperfection, stone lamps carved by Mexican designers, walls dressed in textiles woven by artisan women from the region. Every object in conversation with the territory it inhabits.

To live in Atlas is to walk barefoot and feel the cool of the floors. To smell salt and wood, the rain on the dune and bread from the oven. To hear the fire and the pots with the waves behind. To sit and watch the sky and the life that inhabits the dune. To share aperitivos and let the table run long.
