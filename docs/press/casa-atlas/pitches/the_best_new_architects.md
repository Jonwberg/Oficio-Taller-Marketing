**Design statement:**

The dune is not background — it is ground and shelter.

Casa Atlas sits behind a coastal dune in Cerritos, Baja California Sur — not despite it, but because of it. The dune provides natural shelter and becomes the first architectural move: volumes step with its topography, patios open between them to frame the landscape, and a series of level changes ensures the ocean horizon is visible from every space, including those set deepest into the site.

Earth-toned concrete walls emerge from the ground and open gradually toward the sea. The materials — concrete, local wood, clay — are calibrated to age with the territory, not against it. Light does the rest: morning activates the entrance and the edible garden, afternoon settles into the rooms, evening turns the terraces toward open sky.

The house was designed for a chef and family. Two kitchens are the heart of the program — the upper opens to the terrace, pool, and Pacific; the lower connects to the garden and an outdoor dining space oriented toward the dune. The architecture follows the life that was going to happen in it.
