## Editorial Pitch Letter

Subject: Submission — Casa Atlas / Oficio Taller / Cerritos, Baja California Sur

Dear World Architecture & Design,

We would like to submit Casa Atlas for your consideration — a private residence completed in 2025 in Cerritos, Baja California Sur, designed by Oficio Taller.

The dune organizes the house. Casa Atlas sits behind a coastal dune in Cerritos, Baja California Sur — not despite it, but because of it. The dune provides natural shelter and becomes the first architectural move: volumes step with its topography, patios open between them to frame the landscape, and a series of level changes ensures the ocean horizon is visible from every space in the house, including those set deepest into the site.

Earth-toned concrete walls emerge from the ground and open gradually toward the sea. The materials — concrete, local wood, clay — are calibrated to age with the territory, not against it. Light does the rest: morning activates the entrance and the edible garden, afternoon settles into the rooms, evening turns the terraces toward open sky.

The house was designed for a chef and family. Two kitchens are the heart of the program — the upper opens to the terrace, pool, and Pacific; the lower connects to the garden and an outdoor dining space oriented toward the dune. The architecture follows the life that was going to happen in it.

*Casa Atlas · Cerritos, BCS, México · 458 m² + 90 m² terraces · 2025*
Architecture: Oficio Taller — Marcela González Veloz
Interiors: Oficio Taller + Estudio Homística
Photography: Andrea San Román, Sofía Pollano
