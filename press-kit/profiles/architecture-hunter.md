# Architecture Hunter

## Overview
- **Instagram**: @architecture_hunter (3.2M followers)
- **Related accounts**: @architecture_hunter_awards, @hunterinternationalforum
- **Format**: Instagram-first design media/curatorship
- **Community**: archi-hunters community (19.7K+ members)
- **Reach**: One of the largest architecture curation accounts on Instagram

## Editorial Focus
- **Visual-first curatorship** — the image is everything
- Bold, striking architectural photography
- Projects that photograph dramatically
- Contemporary architecture with strong visual identity
- International scope — projects from around the world
- Mix of large iconic projects and smaller gems with strong visual impact

## Voice & Tone
- Minimal text — the image speaks
- Short, impactful captions
- Hashtag-driven discovery
- Community-oriented (features, reposts)
- Celebratory — showcases the best of architecture visually

## Visual Requirements
- **Instagram-optimized images** (1080x1350 portrait, 1080x1080 square)
- Hero shots with dramatic composition
- Strong contrast, depth, and visual impact
- Golden hour / blue hour photography preferred
- Drone/aerial perspectives valued
- Clean, uncluttered compositions
- 5-7 of the absolute best images (carousel format)

## Content Format
1. Single hero image or carousel (max 10 slides)
2. Short caption: project name, architect, location, photographer
3. Key hashtags for discovery
4. Tag architect and photographer

## What to Emphasize
- **The single most dramatic image** of the project
- Strong geometry and spatial drama
- Light and shadow play
- Integration with landscape (especially dramatic landscapes)
- Scale and proportion
- Unique architectural features that stand out on a scroll

## What to De-emphasize
- Lengthy descriptions or narratives
- Technical drawings and plans
- Construction details
- Interior decoration and styling
- Text-heavy content

## Submission
- DM or tag @architecture_hunter
- Feature-based curation — they select what fits their feed aesthetic
- Photography quality is the primary selection criterion
