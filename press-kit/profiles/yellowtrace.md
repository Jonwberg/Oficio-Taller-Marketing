# Yellowtrace

## Overview
- **Website**: yellowtrace.com.au
- **Founded by**: Dana Tomic Hughes
- **Audience**: Design enthusiasts, architects, interior designers, creatives
- **Base**: Melbourne, Australia
- **Reach**: Global design community, strong social media presence

## Editorial Focus
- **Sensorial narrative** — how spaces feel, not just how they look
- Story-driven features that connect architecture to human experience
- Emphasis on atmosphere, materiality, light, and texture
- Cross-disciplinary: architecture, interiors, art, design, travel
- Values originality, craft, and emotional resonance
- Celebrates projects that challenge convention thoughtfully

## Voice & Tone
- Warm, articulate, passionate
- Personal and opinionated — Dana's curatorial eye is central
- Conversational yet sophisticated
- Celebrates beauty without being superficial
- Emotionally engaged — writes about how spaces make you feel

## Visual Requirements
- Stunning, atmospheric photography is essential
- Images that capture mood, light, and texture
- Detail shots of materials and craftsmanship
- Lifestyle moments within architectural spaces
- Minimum 10-15 high-quality images
- Strong hero image that tells a story

## Content Format
1. Compelling project narrative (600-1200 words)
2. Atmospheric photo essay
3. Designer/architect quotes about intent and process
4. Material and texture close-ups
5. Context shots showing landscape and surroundings

## What to Emphasize
- **Sensory experience**: How light enters, how materials age, how spaces sound
- The emotional journey through the project
- Material honesty and tactile qualities
- Relationship between interior and landscape
- The human story: who lives there, how it's inhabited
- Craft and artisanal details

## What to De-emphasize
- Technical specifications and measurements
- Structural engineering details
- Floor plans (unless they tell a spatial story)
- Construction timelines and budgets
- Generic project descriptions

- Email: hello@yellowtrace.com.au
